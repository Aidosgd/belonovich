Chocolife

App design
2014 year
Visit original
Discription

Chocolife is the largest discount coupon service in Kazakhstan. They provide coupons for all types of goods and services.

Process

I worked on Chocolife with the project manager. At that time mobile apps were new in Kazakhstan market and there wasn’t such definition as UX design. Solutions were based on our own experience and colleagues’ survey and competitor analysis. We prepared a mind map of web site structure, made prototype, drew design and tested hypotheses among colleagues. Work was done in coordination within team and client.

Problems and solutions

There was a problem in viewing current promotions. Some users preferred to see pictures of promotions (like a card), and some to see them in a list. We decided to give a user a choice and made the list version default with option to switch to the card version with pictures.

Result

After the production, we received a lot of positive feedback from users in the App store and Play Market. They noted the convenience of the app. Certainly, the application required improvements, but the client decided to maintain project inside and we hadn’t chance to improve it. Only in 2017 year they launch the second version of the app.

Chocolife was the first major app for me. That year after the release it was downloaded 11 000 times for Android only (now between 500,000 - 1,000,000 install), it is excellent result for our market.

Objective

The objective was to make the first mobile app version (Android & iOS) which has all functions of web site.

KASE

UX/UI design
2016 year
Visit original
Discription

Website redesign of Kazakhstan Stock Exchange.

Work process:

Introduction of the research and analytical stage into the workflow of project development. I initiated the implementation of the research as a separate stage of project development, but we were given just a couple of days for it.

This area is specific, it took a lot of time to investigate and understand it. As a result, we spent a week at the client's office, analyzing the information with him. We carefully studied the stock exchanges of other countries, conducted a survey among the target audience and examined the needs and concerns of each target group. We analyzed the scenarios of each of them.

Problems:

We had serious problems with the prototype, because the client changed the data (content) on pages a lot of times, this leaded modification of pages and back-end. The data changed because of their internal disputes and the company's vague strategy. In the end, we secured the approval of prototype, after a general meeting with management and we no longer had to experiment with the design content.

The approval of the main page took a long time, since the client asked to make glass blocks. We compromised, so the design is different from our original concept. Now I already know how I supposed to convince the client about the irrelevance of the outdated trend.

Result:

We fully changed the structure of the site. Also, we totally changed the main and internal pages based on the data. Separately, we created landings for each target group. The website has been launched in the beta version and received a lot of positive feedback from the client and users; it has been admitted convenient in comparison with the previous version of the website.

Unfortunately, we did not have the opportunity to finalize the web site after launch, as the client decided to make it by own means.

Objectives:

The objective was to redesign the out-dated website taking into account the needs of each target audience, fully update the structure of the site and meet the technical capabilities of the company.

Kompetenz

UX/UI design
2017 year
Visit original
Discription

Redesign of an insurance company website, which has no special advantages over competitors, but the idea was to find out its competitive advantages.

Work process:

From the very beginning, the project was given to another designer. After prototype was approved by client, I got to continue the work. Goals of the website was clear to me, but customer and business problems were’t. I rejected the approved prototype, because it was raw.

I gathered list of questions for clients and received feedback from call-center about clients’ frequently asked question. Benchmarked other insurance websites, got information about which products needed promotions. I was trying to change structure in such a way that users could quickly find the required information.

Due to time shortage, I started to make design based on my research. Client wanted to place logo on the center of the header, convinced that it was modern. Understanding his concern I met with him and we together started working on location of logo and came to conclusion that the best spot was on the left side of the header. After that his concern cleared away.

Next I gathered all our research to presentation, showed animation of the main page, presented it and got approval to work on other pages.

I had to work on close collaboration with fronted developer on website animations.

Problems and solutions:

The advantages and benefits of products were not clear to customers and they did not understand the difference between them. Hence, in product list I put main differences between them, so it will be easier to compare and choose the right product. Also the picture of products were added to attract customers to see all the products.

Result:

While we do not have information how successfully the tasks were accomplished (web-site is under development), but the design concept was approved in the first attempt. I represented it to the client, having explained the differences as improvements of the prototype.

In the debatable cases, I was rearranging the layers in presence of the client. I have explained each decision and its consequences and eventually we achieved a compromise.

For me, it was a good experience of protecting my concept for the client and I have implemented the presentation structure with feature explanations for the client in the company's workflow to use in the future.

We are planning to add google analytics and set goals to see if our hypotheses were right and what can we change to improve the usability. These steps will be taken in website technical maintenance.

Objective

The main objective was to create modern redesign of their website and to change the structure of website. The visual solution also had to be different from competitors. One of the goals was to make sure users understood that website offers to purchase insurance policies.

In the beginning of a project an important task was to approve my design concept that would differ from the prototype.
